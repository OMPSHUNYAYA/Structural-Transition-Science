## üîé What Is Shunyaya Structural Transition Science?

Shunyaya Structural Transition Science (SSTS) is a **deterministic framework**
that evaluates whether a system is **structurally permitted** to transition
between domains ‚Äî most critically, from **physics to chemistry**.

Classical science typically asks:

**Physics asks:**  
Is energy present and conserved?

**Chemistry asks:**  
What reactions occur and at what rates?

**SSTS asks a prior question:**  
Is transition permitted to occur at all?

SSTS:

- does **not** replace physics  
- does **not** replace chemistry  
- does **not** predict outcomes or rates  

It evaluates **permission**, then collapses cleanly back to classical science.

## üéØ Problem Statement ‚Äî Why Energy Is Not Enough

Classical science often assumes:

- sufficient energy implies possible transition  
- legality implies admissibility  
- failure is explained after commitment  

In real systems:

- excitation often does not commit  
- pressure does not always transform  
- identical energies yield different outcomes  
- chemistry sometimes **must not occur**

Energy legality is **not** transition admissibility.

Classical science has no formal layer that decides when energy may safely
become chemistry.

**SSTS fills this missing layer.**

## üß≠ DENY / ABSTAIN / ALLOW

SSTS partitions admissibility into three explicit postures:

- **DENY** ‚Äî transition is structurally forbidden  
- **ABSTAIN** ‚Äî evidence is insufficient to permit commitment  
- **ALLOW** ‚Äî transition is structurally permitted  

**ABSTAIN is intentional.**  
It prevents false certainty near structural boundaries.
